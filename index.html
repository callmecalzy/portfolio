About Me

My name is Michael Angelo Calzado, and my story as an artist began in Iloilo, a journey etched not with a brush, but with the humble point of a pencil. From the moment I could first grip a crayon, I discovered my intrinsic passion for drawing.

The world on screen, a vibrant tapestry of animated wonders—from the timeless grace of Cinderella and Snow White to the exhilarating action of Tom and Jerry, Dragon Ball, One Piece, and Bleach—didn't just entertain me; it ignited my imagination and set my artistic spirit ablaze.

In the nurturing halls of my kindergarten and elementary school, this nascent talent found its first encouragement. My teachers, recognizing the spark in my work, fostered it with praise, transforming simple perfect scores and coveted snacks into the first precious rewards for my effort.

Though my palette was limited to the basics—mostly wax crayons, an HB pencil, and the ubiquitous ballpoint pen—my determination was boundless. I poured my soul onto the paper, eager to present my creations with a fierce sense of pride to my classmates and mentors.

As I entered high school, a new challenge presented itself. The vibrant world of coloring, constrained by the low-quality materials at my disposal, became a source of frustration. Rather than letting this limitation define me, I chose to pivot, to refine, and to specialize.

I turned wholeheartedly to the elegant simplicity of black-and-white drawing. The pencil, particularly the steadfast HB, became an extension of my will, and the mastery of monochrome became my signature. This deliberate focus allowed me to harness the power of light, shadow, and texture with a depth that color often obscures.

The true turning point, however, arrived during my college years. I had never intentionally sought to monetize my passion, but destiny intervened through the simple request of a relative for a portrait. After completing the piece, I decided to share the finished artwork online, and the response was an unanticipated revelation.

As I studied the finished drawing—a face brought to life through my intricate graphite work—a profound question formed in my mind: "Is it my drawing? Why is it so beautiful?"

In that moment of self-discovery, a fledgling artist transformed into a dedicated portrait artist. That single commission was the spark that ignited my professional journey, cementing my focus on the intimate, compelling artistry of the black pencil.

Today, every line, every shade, is a testament to that childhood passion, a continuous pursuit of bringing life and emotion to paper, one meticulous stroke at a time.
